Shastriya Nritya

The 8 Classical Dances of India. Hover to learn more.

Bharatanatyam
Tamil Nadu

The oldest classical dance form. It represents the element of Fire. Known for its fixed upper torso, bent legs, and spectacular footwork.

Read History
Kathak
North India

The art of storytelling (Katha). Famous for its dizzying spins (Chakkars) and intricate rhythmic footwork performed with ghungroos.

Read History
Kathakali
Kerala

A "Story Play" art form. Known for elaborate colorful makeup, costumes, and face masks used to portray gods and demons from epics.

Read History
Odissi
Odisha

Known for its 'Tribhangi' (three-bend) posture, mimicking temple sculptures. It represents the element of Water.

Read History
Kuchipudi
Andhra Pradesh

A dance-drama performance. It combines fast rhythmic footwork with dramatic storytelling and dialogue.

Read History
Manipuri
Manipur

Known for its graceful, gentle movements. The dance depicts the Raslila (Love of Radha-Krishna).

Read History
Mohiniyattam
Kerala

"Dance of the Enchantress". A solo dance by women, characterized by swaying movements like palm trees.

Read History
Sattriya
Assam

Originated in the monasteries (Sattras) of Assam. It was introduced by the saint Srimanta Sankardev.

Chitrakala & Shilpa

Madhubani

Origin: Bihar. Eye-catching geometrical patterns created using fingers, twigs, and brushes.

Warli Art

Origin: Maharashtra. Tribal art using simple geometric shapes (circle, triangle) on red mud walls.

Pattachitra

Origin: Odisha. Cloth-based scroll painting known for intricate details and mythological narratives.

Thanjavur Paintings

Origin: Tamil Nadu. Famous for rich, vivid colors and use of real gold foil overlay.
