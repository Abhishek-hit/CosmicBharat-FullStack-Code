The 4 Pillars of Dharma

Satya Truth

Living authentically. "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth alone triumphs) is the foundation of Indian ethos.

Ahimsa Non-Violence

Respecting all forms of life in thought, word, and deed. Understanding interconnection.

Dharma Righteous Duty

The cosmic order. Doing your duty (Karma) based on your role in family and society.

Karma Action & Effect

The law of cause and effect. Every action has a consequence, empowering you to shape destiny.

Why Do We Live? (The 4 Goals)

Indian culture balances the spiritual and the material.

Dharma
Righteousness

The foundation. Living ethically and doing one's duty.

Artha
Wealth

Economic prosperity is necessary, but it must be earned via Dharma.

Kama
Desire

Emotional and aesthetic pleasure is valid, if controlled by Dharma.

Moksha
Liberation

The ultimate goal. Freedom from the cycle of birth and suffering.

Timeless Wisdom

नात्यन्तं सरलैर्भाव्यं...
"Do not be too straight. Go and see the forest."

Straight trees are cut first. In life, being too naive leads to exploitation. Be kind, but smart.

विद्या मित्रं प्रवासेषु...
"Knowledge is your friend on a journey."

Wealth can be lost, but skills (Vidya) stay with you everywhere. Invest in yourself.
