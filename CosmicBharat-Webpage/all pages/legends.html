1630 - 1680 CE
The Father of Indian Navy

Chhatrapati Shivaji

"Har Har Mahadev!"

He challenged the mighty Mughal Empire with a handful of soldiers. Pioneering Guerilla Warfare (Ganimi Kava), he built forts on land and sea to protect Swarajya. He was not just a king, but a master strategist who prioritized the welfare of farmers and women.

1540 - 1597 CE
The Lion of Mewar

Maharana Pratap

"My head bows only to Eklingji."

While all other kings surrendered to Akbar, Pratap stood alone. In the Battle of Haldighati, he fought with a spear weighing 80kg. He lived in the forest, ate grass rotis, but never compromised his freedom. He is the ultimate symbol of self-respect.

321 - 297 BCE
The First Emperor

Chandragupta Maurya

"United We Stand."

From a commoner to the Emperor of Akhand Bharat. Guided by the genius of Chanakya, he defeated the Greek general Seleucus Nicator and the corrupt Nanda dynasty. He established a unified administration that ran from Afghanistan to Bengal.

The Youngest Hero

Veer Abhimanyu

"Courage needs no age."
