The Chronology of
Bharatvarsha

Explore the lineage of the greatest Emperors who ruled the subcontinent from the dawn of civilization to the modern era.

Satyuga: The Age of Truth

The era of the first kings, where Dharma stood on four legs.

Vaivasvata Manu

The First King of Humanity

According to Puranas, he is the progenitor of the current humanity (Manvantara). He saved the Vedas and the seeds of life during the great deluge (Pralaya) with the help of the Matsya Avatar.

Capital: Ayodhya (Early tradition)
Lineage: Suryavansha (Solar Dynasty)

Treta Yuga

The era of massive sacrifices and the Ideal King.

Chakravartin Mandhata

Emperor of the Seven Continents

An ancestor of Lord Rama. It is said the sun never set on his empire. He was a master of warfare and administration.

Capital: Ayodhya
Known For: Protecting sages from Ravana (early years)

Sri Rama

Maryada Purushottam (The Ideal Man)

The 7th Avatar of Vishnu. His reign (Ram Rajya) is considered the benchmark for perfect governance, justice, and prosperity.

Capital: Ayodhya
Philosophy: Dharma above all

Dwapara Yuga

The era of complex politics and the Mahabharata.

Samrat Yudhishthira

Dharmaraj (King of Righteousness)

The eldest Pandava. After the Kurukshetra war, he performed the Ashvamedha Yajna and ruled the entire subcontinent from Indraprastha/Hastinapura.

Capital: Indraprastha / Hastinapura
Dynasty: Kuru (Lunar Dynasty)

Kali Yuga (History)

The recorded history of Magadha, Maurya, Gupta, and Maratha empires.

Chandragupta Maurya

Founder of Mauryan Empire (321 BCE)

With Chanakya's help, he united fractured kingdoms into the first pan-Indian empire, defeating the Greeks (Seleucus Nicator).

Capital: Pataliputra (Patna)
Extent: Afghanistan to Bengal

Samudragupta

The Napoleon of India (335 CE)

A warrior-poet who never lost a battle. His reign marked the beginning of the Golden Age of India.

Dynasty: Gupta
Symbol: Garuda
